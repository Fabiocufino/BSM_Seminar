The Higgs Mechanism

Toy Model: The Abelian Higgs Model

  • Simplified verison of SM with \(U(1)\) symmetry: \(A_{\mu}(x) \rightarrow e^{i\theta}A_{\mu}(x)\)
  • Consider \(U(1)\) gauge theory with single gauge field: Photon \(A_{\mu}\)

\[ L = -\frac{1}{4}F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu} \;\;\;\;\;\;\;\; \text{with} \;\;\;\;\;\;\;\; F_{\mu\nu} = \partial_\nu A_\mu - \partial_\mu A_\nu \]

  • Local \(U(1)\) gauge invariance: \(A_{\mu}(x) \rightarrow A_{\mu}(x) - \partial_{\mu}\eta(x)\)

Mass term:

\[ +\frac{1}{2} m^2 A_{\mu} A^{\mu} \]

  • Violates the local gauge invariance.

The Higgs Mechanism

Extension of the model

  • Single complex scalar field \(\phi\) that couples to the photon \[ L = -\frac{1}{4}F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu} + |D_\mu \phi|^2 - V(\phi) \]
    • \(D_\mu = \partial_\mu - ieA_\mu\)
    • \(V(\phi) = \mu^2 |\phi|^2 + \lambda |\phi|^4\)

\(\mathcal{L}\) is invariant under:

  • Global \(U(1)\) rotations: \(\;\; A_{\mu}(x) \rightarrow e^{i\theta}A_{\mu}(x), \;\; \phi \rightarrow e^{i\theta} \phi\)

  • Local gauge transformations: \(\;\; A_\mu \rightarrow A_\mu - \partial_\mu \eta(x), \;\;\;\; \phi \rightarrow e^{-ie\eta(x)} \phi\)

Introduction to the Weinberg-Salam Model

Weinberg-Salam Model

  • Extends the Abelian Higgs model to an \(SU(2)_L×U(1)_Y\) gauge theory.
  • Introduces three \(SU(2)_L\) gauge bosons \(W_\mu^I\) (I = 1, 2, 3) and one \(U(1)_Y\) gauge boson \(B_\mu\).
  • The kinetic enerigy terms: \[ L = -\frac{1}{4}W^I_{\mu\nu}W^{I\mu\nu} - \frac{1}{4}B_{\mu\nu}B^{\mu\nu} \]
    • \(W^I_{\mu\nu} = \partial_\nu W^I_\mu - \partial_\mu W^I_\nu + g \epsilon^{IJK} W^J_\mu W^K_\nu\)
    • \(B_{\mu\nu} = \partial_\nu B_\mu - \partial_\mu B_\nu\)

Scalar Potential and VEV

Slide 7: Gauge Boson Masses Calculation

Gauge Boson Masses

\[ M^2 \sim \frac{1}{2} (0, v) \left( \frac{1}{2} g \tau \cdot W_\mu + \frac{1}{2} g' B_\mu \right)^2 \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ v \end{pmatrix}. \]

  • The gauge bosons mix to form the physical gauge fields:

\[ W^\pm_\mu = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(W_\mu^1 \mp iW_\mu^2) \] \[ Z_\mu = \frac{gW_\mu^3 - g'B_\mu}{\sqrt{g^2 + g'^2}} \equiv -sin\theta_W B_\mu + cos\theta_W W^3_\mu \] \[ A_\mu = \frac{g'W_\mu^3 + gB_\mu}{\sqrt{g^2 + g'^2}} \equiv cos\theta_W B_\mu + sin\theta_W W^3_\mu \]

The weak mixing angle \(\theta_W\) is defined by: \(\sin \theta_W = \frac{g'}{\sqrt{g^2 + g'^2}}\)

The relationship with the electromagnetic coupling \(e\) is: \(e = g \sin \theta_W = g' \cos \theta_W\)

Fermion Mass Generation

Leptons can be coupled in a gauge invariant manner to the \(SU(2)_L × U(1)_Y\) gauge fields

\[ \mathcal{L}_{\text{lepton}} = i \bar{e}_R \gamma^\mu \left( \partial_\mu + i \frac{g'}{2} Y_e B_\mu \right) e_R + i \bar{L}_L \gamma^\mu \left( \partial_\mu + i \frac{g}{2} \tau \cdot W_\mu + i \frac{g'}{2} Y_L B_\mu \right) L_L \]

Slide 11: Importance of Mass Generation without Violating Gauge Invariance

Importance of Gauge-Invariant Mass Generation

  • The Higgs mechanism ensures gauge invariance while giving mass to particles.
  • Yukawa couplings generate fermion masses without violating gauge symmetry.
  • This avoids flavor-changing neutral currents, ensuring the model’s consistency with observed physics.
